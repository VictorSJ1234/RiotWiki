Synopsis

League of Legends or also known as League is a free to play Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA) and team-oriented strategy game, it is founded by Brandon Beck and Marc Merrill in 2006 with a clear vision of supporting or prioritizing players experience. League climbs the largest e-sports community in the world and supported by hundreds and millions of fans to watch annually as their best-loved professional teams and players compete for Championships, with salaried pro gamers produce at the very best ranges of opposition with hundreds of thousands of dollars at the line.


Gameplay

The two teams of five powerful champions have only one goal and that is to break the Nexus on the enemy team. The Nexus is where the player’s allied minions will spawn and behind of the Nexus is the fountain wherein player can rapidly regenerate his health and mana, also can access the Shop. To reach the opposing Nexus, the team must clear at least one lane. Turrets and inhibitors are defense structures that block the enemies’ progress. Each lane features three turrets and one inhibitor, with two turrets guarding each Nexus. Turrets deliver damage to opposing minions and champions while also providing their team with limited visibility due to the Fog of War. To dodge damage and rush ahead, the players should attack these structures with minions in front of you. A Turret surrounds each Inhibitor to keep it safe. Super minions will spawn in that lane for several minutes after being destroyed. The Inhibitor will revive after that, and Super Minions will stop spawning. The jungle, which lies between the lanes, is home to neutral beasts and jungle vegetation. The Drakes and Baron Nashor are the two most powerful beasts. Killing these units provides your team with distinct benefits and can even swing the game around.

Sample gameplay of League of Legends

The optimum squad composition for the game consists of five lanes. Each lane caters to specific champions and roles; try them all or stick to the one that speaks to you. Top lane champions are the team's toughest individual fighters. It is their responsibility to defend their lane while concentrating on the opponent team's strongest members. Junglers exist to hunt. They sneak between lanes with stealth and skill, keeping a watchful eye on the most crucial neutral monsters and waiting for an opponent to drop their guard. Mid laners are high-burst damage champions who can do it all, both solo and with a team. Combat is a perilous dance for them, and they're always looking for methods to outplay their opponents. Bot lane champions are the team's dynamite. They must be protected early on as valuable cargo before gathering enough gold and experience to propel the team to victory. Support champions are team guardians. They help keep teammates alive and primarily focus on setting up kills, protecting their teammate in bot lane until they become stronger.

The creators want to release a new agent approximately once each act. New agents take 12–15 months to build, and their design is centered on issues such as what's lacking from the game's existing playstyles, whether there are any potential thematics that provide significant opportunities, and what's required for greater game health.

Sample gameplay of League of Legends

As the game proceeds, Champions gain experience and wealth to level up and purchase more powerful things. Overpowering the opponent team and destroying their base requires staying on top of these two aspects. Champions level up as they gain experience, unlocking or strengthening skills and increasing their base stats. Kill enemy units and champions, assist in a kill, and destroy defense buildings to get experience. The in-game money used to purchase equipment for your champion is gold. Kill enemy units and champions, aid in a kill, destroy defense structures, and equip gold income equipment to earn gold. The Shop is where you may use your gold to buy and sell stuff. It's only available at the Fountain. Champions can have up to seven items and five core skills at once. Choosing the right ability order, summoner spells, and item composition for your champion will aid the team's success.

Cosmetics

There are now 235 Chroma skins in League of Legends. Chroma skins are simply distinct skin modifications that can improve and differentiate the skin. There are around 2750 Chroma Skins in League of Legends right now.

Riot Games has also improved their Chroma game in the last six months. They frequently add nearly as many chromas to these skins as they do to conventional skins. When most new 1350 RP skins are introduced, they now include Chroma options as well.

Chromas are not included in the overall number of skins in League of Legends since, while they are distinctive, they are merely a duplicate of another skin that cannot be used without it. Chroma skins can be purchased after purchasing a specific skin and can only be utilized when that skin is equipped.

There are seven different price categories for store skins:

  • 390 RP
  • 520 RP
  • 750 RP
  • 975 RP
  • 1350 RP (Epic Skin)
  • 1820 RP (Legendary Skin)
  • 3250 RP (Ultimate Skin)

Legendary and Ultimate skins are the most advanced, with a variety of visual and auditory elements. However, there are just a few Legendary skins and a few Ultimate skins. There are 26 skins that are no longer available for purchase, 55 rare skins, and 309 Legacy skins.

Prestige Skins, on the other hand, may only be obtained through in-game events. These skins cannot be purchased with RP and must be unlocked using Prestige Points or Event Points obtained through Event passes. Orange Essence or Riot Points will not unlock Prestige Skins (RP).

Promotional skins are the final category. They can only be obtained by participating in certain promotions, such as obtaining Riot Girl Tristana through their Facebook promotion.

Characters

The player can choose for over 140 champions that is fit to his desired role. The game has six different categories for champions:

  • Assassin - Move rapidly while dealing massive damage to a single target. Quick fighting and jumping to safety are the finest uses for these heroes.
  • Fighter - These champions have a lot of damage dealing and taking abilities. Fighters excel in long-duration battles.
  • Mages - These champions excel in long-distance battles. Mages may do a lot of damage while remaining invulnerable to attacks if they are sufficiently protected.
  • Marksmen - Like mages, Marksmen are best used while doing damage at a distance.
  • Support - The primary function of support characters is to magnify the strengths and abilities of their teammates.
  • Tank – Just like fighters, Tanks are best deployed as a "damage sponge."

Due to the numerous playable champions in the game, one character per category is presented as an example on how these roles are played.

Master Yi (Assassin)

Master Yi has tempered his body and honed his mind to the point where thought and action are practically interchangeable. Though he only uses violence as a last resort, the grace and speed of his blade assures that any conflict is resolved quickly. As one of the last living practitioners of the Ionian art of Wuju, Yi has dedicated his life to upholding his people's legacy, analyzing potential new followers using the Seven Lenses of Insight to select the most deserving.

Skills

Double Strike (Passive)

Master Yi strikes twice after every few basic attacks.

Alpha Strike (Q)

Master Yi teleports across the battlefield at breakneck speed, causing physical damage to several units along the way while remaining untargetable. Alpha Hit can critically strike creatures and deal additional physical damage. Alpha Strike's cooldown is reduced by basic attacks.

Meditate (W)

Master Yi rejuvenates his body by concentrating his mind, restoring health and reducing damage for a brief period of time. For each second, he channels, Master Yi will acquire stacks of Double Strike and halt the remaining duration on Wuju Style and Highlander.

Wuju Style (E)

On basic attacks, adds extra true damage.

Highlander (R)

Master Yi moves with incredible agility, improving his Move Speed and Attack Speed while also making him immune to any slowing effects. Highlander's duration is extended if Champion kills or assists while active. On a kill or assist, he reduces the cooldown for his other abilities passively.

Nasus (Fighter)

Nasus is a powerful, jackal-headed Ascended creature from ancient Shurima who is revered as a deity by the desert people. He was a fiercely clever keeper of knowledge and a master strategist whose wisdom lead Shurima's ancient empire to grandeur for millennia. He withdrew into self-imposed exile after the fall of the empire, becoming little more than a legend. He has returned now that the old city of Shurima has risen again, determined to guarantee it never falls again.

Skills

Soul Eater (Passive)

Nasus absorbs his opponent's spiritual force, increasing his Life Steal.

Siphoning Strike (Q)

If Nasus slays his opponent, he deals damage and increases the power of his future Siphoning Strikes.

Wither (W)

Nasus slows down an enemy champion's Move Speed and Attack Speed over time.

Spirit Fire (E)

Nasus summons a spirit flame that deals damage and lowers the Armor of opponents who stand in its path.

Fury of the Sands (R)

Nasus conjures a spirit flame that inflicts damage and reduces the Armor of those who come into contact with it.

Ahri (Mage)

Ahri is a vastaya who can mold magic into orbs of raw energy and is inextricably linked to Runeterra's hidden power. She enjoys playing with her prey's emotions before swallowing their life force. Despite her predatory nature, Ahri retains empathy as each soul she takes provides her with glimpses of memories.

Skills

Essence Theft (Passive)

When Ahri uses her abilities to attack 9 foes, her next ability heals her for each enemy hit.

Orb of Deception (Q)

Ahri launches her orb out and then pulls it back, causing magic damage out and true damage back.

Fox-Fire (W)

Ahri receives a short burst of Move Speed and launches three fox-fires at adjacent opponents. Charm (E)

Charm (E)

When Ahri blows a kiss at an enemy, it hurts and charms them, disabling their mobility abilities and allowing them to wander harmlessly towards her.

Spirit Rush (R)

Ahri sprints forward and fires essence bolts at adjacent opponents, dealing damage. Spirit Rush can be cast up to three times before going on cooldown, and while taking down enemy champions, it earns additional recasts.

Jinx (Marksman)

Jinx is a maniacal and rash criminal from Zaun who lives to cause mayhem with little regard for the consequences. She unleashes the loudest blasts and brightest explosions from her armory of lethal weapons, leaving a trail of mayhem and panic in her wake. Jinx despises boredom and delights in bringing her own brand of chaos wherever she goes.

Skills

Get Excited! (Passive)

Jinx's Move Speed and Attack Speed are greatly improved anytime she assists in the killing or destruction of an enemy champion's epic jungle monster or building.

Switcheroo! (Q)

Jinx alters her basic attacks by switching between her minigun Pow-Pow and her rocket launcher Fishbones. Pow-Pow attacks offer Attack Speed, but Fishbones attacks deal area of effect damage, have a longer range, but drain Mana and attack slower.

Zap! (W)

Jinx fires a blast with Zapper, her shock pistol, that damages the first enemy struck, slowing and revealing it.

Flame Chompers! (E)

Jinx launches a barrage of snare grenades that explode after 5 seconds, setting foes ablaze. Enemy champions who walk over Flame Chompers will be bitten and rooted in place.

Super Mega Death Rocket! (R)

Jinx launches a super rocket across the map, dealing damage along the way. When the rocket collides with an opponent champion, it explodes, causing damage to it and nearby enemies based on their missing health.

Bard (Support)

Bard, a visitor from beyond the stars, is a serendipitous agent who strives to establish a balance in which life can withstand chaos' indifference. Many Runeterrans write songs about his remarkable nature, but they all agree that the cosmic wanderer is lured to powerful magical treasures. Surrounded by a joyful choir of helpful spirit meeps, it's hard to misinterpret Bard's activities as malicious, as he always acts in the best interests of the greater good... in his own peculiar way.

Skills

Traveler’s Call (Passive)

Bard attracts lesser spirits to help him deal extra magic damage with his basic attacks. When Bard has a sufficient number of Chimes, his meeps will additionally cause damage in a specific area and slow foes that are hit.
Ancient chimes arrive at random for Bard to collect. These provide experience, mana restoration, and out-of-combat Move Speed.

Cosmic Binding (Q)

Bard shoots a missile that slows down the first enemy hit before continuing onward. It will stun the first victim if it hits a wall; if it hits another adversary, it will stun both of them.

Caretaker's Shrine (W)

Reveals a healing shrine that charges up for a short period before fading and speeding up the first ally who touches it.

Magical Journey (E)

A portal is opened by Bard in neighboring terrain. By traveling inside the portal, both allies and adversaries can take a one-way trip through that area.

Tempered Fate (R)

All champions, minions, monsters, and turrets impacted by Bard's spirit energy arc are thrown into stasis for a short time.

Aatrox (Tank)

Aatrox and his kin, once revered as Shurima's defenders against the Void, would soon become an even bigger menace to Runeterra, and were only slain by cunning mortal sorcery. But, after decades in captivity, Aatrox was the first to reclaim his freedom, corrupting and altering those who dared to use the magical weapon containing his essence. He now travels Runeterra in a terrible imitation of his original shape, seeking apocalyptic and long-overdue retribution with stolen flesh.

Skills

Deathbringer Stance (Passive)

Aatrox's next basic attack occasionally deals bonus physical damage and heals him, depending on the target's maximum health.

The Darkin Blade (Q)

Aatrox strikes with his greatsword, inflicting physical damage. He may swing three times, each time affecting a different location.

Infernal Chains (W)

Aatrox slams his fist into the ground, inflicting damage on the first enemy he hits. Champions and huge monsters must flee the impact zone immediately, or they will be dragged back to the center and suffer damage.

Umbral Dash (E)

When opposing champions are damaged, Aatrox heals passively. He dashes in a direction after activation.

World Ender (R)

Fearing surrounding enemy minions, Aatrox transforms into his demonic form, increasing attack damage, increased healing, and improved Move Speed. This effect is amplified if he scores a takedown.


Trivia

  • The Game's Design Director is named Zilean. Tom "Zileas" Cadwell, a creative director at Riot Games, inspired Zilean, the legendary Chronokeeper. Cadwell has an outstanding resume, including a computer science degree from MIT and work on Warcraft III: The Frozen Throne and World of Warcraft at Blizzard.
  • A 14th-Century Italian Knight is mentioned in the game. The famous Italian fencing master Fiore dei Liberi had a large influence on martial arts, due mainly to his illuminated manuscripts which have survived for over 600 years. In League of Legends, the character Fiora uses fencing as her fighting style and she has a very similar name.
  • Tristana's Buster Shot is a Mega Man tribute. Another blue action hero with a buster shot you might be familiar with. The likeness is uncanny.
